  • A fashion creation defines its identity once it takes shape on the body of a woman, by accompanying, prolonging and highlighting the movement. Isabelle Ballu designs for those who totally live their look, to the extent of mix-mingling it with their personalities.

    'I can't imagine a woman like a butterfly, pinned dead in a glass case, her name on a tag below. I want her unleashed, giving free flight to her beauty and power of charm and seduction'.

    The very concept of the collection for next winter lies within the versatility of possibilities and hence the absence of limit and boundaries.The same piece can assume different identites, always entirely feminine but secret or extrovert, effervecent or austere, according to the mood, the moment in time, before, after..

    A dress, so wide as to seem more of a cover, a protection, suddenly hugs the body, fastened by a system of belts and bras. The tails 'of a sober and strictly cut overcoat disapear and allow an entirely different perception of the silhouette. Vast capes are severely girdled at the waist, in dramatize the instantaneous glimpses of actions by accentuating their dynamics. Why not freeze these movements when at their most harmonious? The gesture of drawing aside the layers of a coat in order to slip hands into the pockets of her pants brings out a perfectly 'gargonne' attitude. With just this intention, straps drape the front of a coat and make place for a new interpretation of the eternal 'masculin/feminin' theme. It is no longer the trans-gender borrowing in the other sex's wardrobe, but more the distinctly masculine attitude in dress, which inspire the designs. Seemingly wide and unstructured coats reveal tight liners in vivid red, cognac and bronze, clinging to the shape of the body. Chains and belts maintain apparently over-sized pants and skirts in place on the hips.

    The natural flow of the fabric allows glimpses, peeks of what is happening beneath: the deep decollete of a dress is partially hidden by a 'curtain' cape. The 'bare skin/dressed skin' confrontation is particularly vivid in the bolero-dresses, a 'window' placed in the back, opening down to the waist. The collar and necklines rise high in the front, then fall open in the rear, revealing the lower back. Jackets feature a high center-slit and are worn tight, tuxedo-style, upon the naked skin.

    The surface of the fabrics come alive with the luminous movement created by lacerations and boucle knits, reminiscent of astrakhan. Various jacquard and raised patterns provide a diversity in the range of blacks: mat, shiny, - satiny or iced, mix-matched on the same silhouette. Amber, cognac, brown and rust alternatively interpret sensations of 􀀊ana cold, set off by touches of deep violet and blood red. Antique gold discretely highlights these dark shades of color. A distinct sensuality is revealed by the sheer and supple Italian woollens, the black and chocolate velvet and the silks, used for blouses and dresses. Faded gold and black lurex jacquards accentuate the structure of the cut, outlined by raised stitching around the bodice and the waistline.

    The colors and materials used for the wided range of accessories perfectly rhyme with the collection. Isabelle Ballu designs the bags with the precise idea of 'a tiny locked and shut house, nevertheless telling a lot about its owner. Even alone, the bags immediately start to reveal their very singular identities'. Coordinated with the outfits, the purses and bags are in leather with variations of mat, shiny, gloss and suede. Edges and corners are folded upwards and thus 'frozen'. Double-volumes, apparently enclenched together, feature contrasting hues of color such as violeVblood red and violeVcognac. The shoulder straps seem to unravel and snap, victims of fictive wear and use.

    The belts and jewelry further develop the breaking cord theme, which, despite a preliminary 'brut' appearance, superbly accords with the sophistication of the dresses. Animalistic shapes come alive on necklaces, earings and broches, and a line of snails inching along a collar perfectly illustrates the 'frozen' movement. Scarves in mink, joined in two-tone color combinations, are worn tightly enlaced around the neck.

    Head-dress in feathers, taking on shapes reminiscent of the 40's as well as the era of punk, are created in exclusive collaboration with the houses Michel and Lemarie.

    Beyond a devoted quest for a valorisation of the silhouette, this collection for next winter encourages to bring out the unexpected in a personality. A true message to the woman who will wear the designs, a conversation of fashion, this season launches the new Isabelle Ballu logo, embroidered inside the garment: an eye captured amidst two stylized letters. The desire to voice and pursue a dialogue, the exchange between the interior and the exterior .. an eye wide-open on the world, fearing not to look within and beyond.
